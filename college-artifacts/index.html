Nick Cassleman - College artifacts

I went to college at the University of Chicago, but it ended up being a bad fit. My classes demanded too much work of me, too much reading, too much theory. I learned to avoid school work that bored me and instead focused my attention on the fun stuff.

I switched directions a bunch before designing my own curriculum as part of the Interdiscplinary Studies in the Humanities department. To study "Interactive Media and Game Design," I combined classes from creative writing, psychology, computer science, visual arts, and critical game theory. My senior thesis was a game about exploring affect theory through gameplay.

I also did hella clubs. I was a leader in clubs for aikido, Scav Hunt, circus, game design, queer life, dorm council, Dean's Advisory Council. They gave me a fancy award for it when I graduated. Yes, this is me bragging.

From those times, I kept a few of the files I liked and threw away the rest.
