Nick Cassleman - College artifacts

I went to college at the University of Chicago, but it ended up being a bad fit. My classes demanded too much work of me, too much reading, too much theory. I learned to avoid school work that bored me and instead focused my attention on the fun stuff.

I switched directions a bunch before designing my own curriculum as part of the Interdiscplinary Studies in the Humanities department. To study "Interactive Media and Game Design," I combined classes from creative writing, psychology, computer science, visual arts, and critical game theory. My senior thesis was a game about exploring affect theory through gameplay.

I also did hella clubs. I was a leader in clubs for aikido, Scav Hunt, circus, game design, queer life, dorm council, Dean's Advisory Council. They gave me a fancy award for it when I graduated. Yes, this is me bragging.

From those times, I kept a few of the files I liked and threw away the rest.

Posters

I made informational posters for a few clubs. They were fine.

Oils

I took a painting class and learned a bit about painting in oils. I didn't really know how to draw or make art outside my personal cartoon styles, so I relied heavily on abstract. (This has been a common theme in my life, see the stylized figures from Drink n' Draw.)

  1. I guess this one was supposed to be dirty and sinister and layered. During critiques, I set my painting up on a plinth with my dirty rags behind a temporary well used for everyone else's paintings. I made everyone wedge themselves into the tight, dark space to look at it. It was a cheap trick.
  2. This one was an abstraction of a staircase outside the art room. I was playing with chromatic grays (grays that have bits of color in them).

Watercolors

Watercolors freak me out because I love to overwork things and that's a big no-no with watercolor. Still, a friend asked if I could illustrate a pair of articles for the Southside Weekly magazine after seeing a poster I'd made for taco night watercolor I made for Z&H Market Cafe.

  • An illustration for Fish Sauce. I draw a little scene depicting a fish-based divination which doesn't have anything to do with the poem but I thought looked cool.
  • An illustration for Spokane, by my friend Hannah. She had an aunt and uncle there who owned a goat farm in Spokane near where I grew up in WA. The pose is inspired by Joanna Newsom's Ys; the hills in the background are of the Palouse, the land around Spokane; and I guess I thought a penknife was a pen that actually had a knife under the cap.
  • My regular sandwich shop, Z&H Market Cafe, had a weekly taco night for a while. One time, I bought a bunch of supplies for fresh margaritas and made the staff and my friends drinks for the night. I painted this for Margarita Night.

Song

I dabbled in music composition software during high school. During that time, I was mediocre at playing the viola and had taught myself a bit of music theory, guitar, and piano. In college, I took a music composition course and wrote this for an assignment. My friend Thomas performed it, and I think he did a great job. Unfortunately, I don't think my teacher thought I had done as great of a job with the piece. I forgot what I named it, and I've lost the sheet music.
