Nick Cassleman - Amateur poetry

Wowie! Never thought these would see the light of day!

When I say that I am a poet, I don't mean these poems. I am a poetic see-er of the world, a poetic thinker. I started writing poetry for myself as a practice in expression, not for the production of poems. These were drawn like doodles: personal, imperfect, destined for the compost heap. By accident, I've written a few that I like enough to share.

Journey into poetry

My journey toward writing and appreciating poetry is long and ongoing. I graduated with a degree in the liberal arts hating it! Poetry always felt pretentious to me, inaccessible. After college, I invited an old professor, Peter O'Leary, out to lunch and asked him why I should care about poetry. In an afternoon, I was convinced. Not to like poetry, but to work at developing my own relationship with it. I don't have to like every kind of poetry, just like I don't have to like every kind of painting. We talked about the root of amateur being amare, to love, and how that differs from being a professional. I can write poetry for the love of the process rather than because I'm trying to make something of value for someone else.

Poems for many occasions

Poems don't have to be a big deal! They can be written in the moment, reworked, and thrown away in as long as it takes to eat a wet sandwich. You don't have to share them if you don't want. You can give them as gifts on birthdays and celebrations. They sometimes live on beats and in tune. They can be found by mistake talking of things without any stake.

Cascade

When the universe
pours itself through you,
open your mouth wider
and let it overflow

Flex and reflex

How deep must the flame have scorched
That no water could soothe
Nor salve could penetrate

That long after, I would flinch
When the warmth from your skin
Glanced my white scars

Between

Love is not a point

Love is a line
love is a triangle
love is a rectangle
bent and skewed

No love story
has singular perspective,
the impossible coin
bearing only one side

glass bells

walking under
frozen trees
loose leaf ice
rings
glass-bell sweet
beneath my heavy
feet

My heart slows underwater

I did not ask to wake today,
but when I do,
I emerge
in slow quiet,
the breath its own pace

Clarity soon clears
like morning mist,
burned by the fire
of a heart in love,
by winds of a restless mind

I bid farewell the peace
and prepare for the weight
of burdens from today,
collecting like burs
tenacious and thorned

impulse

when you hear a flie
buzzing
where do you reach?

Leavened

I saw you first
From far away
Captured your image
And ground it into meal

It mixed with yeast
Time and water
Growing its own life
In the pit of my belly

Live wire

Night never stills in my city.
Its deep and steady current
Hums above and drones below
And between it illuminates
The hours bewitched

Lye-slick star

We flee the breath
Of winter's languid sun,
Over crests coerced,
mirrors red and bright;

Through pale gases,
From underearth, above,
Seeps darkling din
Pulled prisms elastic;

Doused salt, twilight slate,
Caught glint of stolen hues,
Until dawn refreshed
Spills ink like amber tar

Shade in the window

My shadow, a fly
balancing
at the corner
of your eye

Submission

The hue of today
Slips through my fingers
Quicksand slick
Leaves the taste
Of dust in my mouth
As I sink

The well

Can I call it a gift
these poems
that his woundings write

They pour from my fingers
from a place beyond my mind
the cavern much deeper

The way our threads wove

It was the night,
homeward I rode
the twilight breeze;
it snagged a loosened seam
and threatened to unravel
the order I had woven;
I already finished
our story,
its ending was neatly hemmed

tomorrow

to wake up
and love the day

translation

whose crests
rest different
on every horizon,
from any perspective

its hazy shape
coerced into words:

dreamlike cells,
themselves,

Two sides of me

Hope is a desert hot
relentless dry and uncontrollable

It is the moon's cool light
illuminating a path toward dawn

Zero sum

I am
who are
the parts
