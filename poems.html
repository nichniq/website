Cascade

When the universe
pours itself through you,
open your mouth wider
and let it overflow

Flex and reflex

How deep must the flame have scorched
That no water could soothe
Nor salve could penetrate

That long after, I would flinch
When the warmth from your skin
Glanced my white scars

Geometry

Love is not a point

Love is a line
love is triangle
love is a rectangle
askew at its corner

Your love story,
with its neat point,
is an impossible coin
bearing only one side

glass bells

walking under
frozen trees
loose leaf ice
rings
glass-bell sweet
beneath my heavy
feet

My heart slows underwater

I did not ask to wake today,
but when I do,
I will emerge in a quiet
born of softness,
breath at its own pace

The clarity soon clears
like morning mist,
burned by the fire
of a heart in love,
by winds of a restless mind

I bid farewell the peace
and prepare for the weight
of burdens from today,
collecting like burs
tenacious and thorned

impulse

when you hear a flie
buzzing
where do you reach?

Live wire

Night never stills in my city.
Its deep and steady current
Hums above and drones below
And between it illuminates
Even the hours bewitched

Lye-slick star

We flee the breath
Of winter's languid sun,
Over crests coerced,
mirrors red and bright;

Through pale gases,
From underearth, above,
Seeps darkling din
Pulled prisms elastic;

Doused salt, twilight slate,
Caught glint of stolen hues,
Until dawn refreshed
Spills ink like amber tar

Sand

The sandy hue of today
Slips through my fingers
Quicksand slick
Leaving the taste
Of salt in my mouth
And I sink

Shade in the window

My shadow, a fly
balancing
at the corner
of your eye

The well

Can I call it a gift
these poems
that his woundings write

They pour from my fingers
from a place beyond my mind
a cavern much deeper

The way our threads wove

It was the night,
and home I rode
the twilight breeze,
it snagged a loosened seam
and threatened to unravel
the order I had woven
of our story
and the ending
I thought might neatly hem

tomorrow

to wake up
and love the day

translation

poetry as the fog,
whose crests
rest different
on every horizon,
from any perspective

suggesting haze,
the shape of a word:

its dreamlike cells,
themselves,
a phrase on the lips;
a gesture,
its fingers

Two sides of me

Hope is a desert hot
relentless dry and uncontrollable

It is the moon's cool light
illuminating a path toward dawn

Zero sum

I am
who are
the parts
eating within me
starved, nourished
